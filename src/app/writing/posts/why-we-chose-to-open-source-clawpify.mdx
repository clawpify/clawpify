AI is changing the landscape of software development. With agentic coding, there is less and less reason to believe that simply building software is the moat. Code is being commoditized.

What matters more now is distribution and craft. Distribution determines whether anyone finds your product at all. Craft determines whether what you build is actually worth using, trusting, and coming back to.

As that shift happens, we think more products should be open, inspectable, and easy to run without asking permission from a vendor. That is a big part of why we open sourced Clawpify.

Too much of the AI commerce stack is becoming a black box. Teams are asked to trust scores they cannot inspect, workflows they cannot adapt, and recommendations they cannot verify. That is the opposite of how infrastructure for a new market should behave.

## Open source builds trust

When a merchant connects their catalog, they should be able to understand what the product is doing with that data. Open source makes that possible. It lets people inspect how prompts are run, how outputs are evaluated, and how recommendations are generated.

That transparency matters even more in AI commerce because model behavior changes quickly. If the system is visible, people can keep pace with those changes instead of waiting for a vendor to explain them after the fact.

## We want people to extend it

Every store is different. Some care about Shopify metadata. Others care about structured content, feeds, collections, reviews, or custom taxonomies. We do not want Clawpify to be useful only for one narrow workflow.

By open sourcing the project, we make it easier for teams to plug in their own data sources, add their own scoring logic, and adapt the product to the way they actually operate.

## We want people to self-host it

We also want people to be able to self-host Clawpify themselves. Some teams want full control over their infra, data, prompts, and deployment workflow. Others just prefer owning the stack they rely on.

Open source makes that possible. If you want to run it locally, fork it, deploy it inside your own environment, or customize it for a specific workflow, you should be able to do that without friction.

## The category is still early

AI commerce is still being invented in real time. Best practices are not settled yet. The fastest way to learn is to build in public, let people challenge assumptions, and improve the product with real feedback.

Open source gives that process a home. Instead of pretending we already know the final shape of AI visibility tooling, we can iterate with the people who are trying to solve the problem today.

## Why this matters for Clawpify

We want Clawpify to become a durable layer in the AI commerce stack, not just a polished demo. That means making it easier to trust, easier to audit, and easier to build on top of.

Open sourcing Clawpify is a product decision as much as a philosophy decision. It aligns the way we build with the kind of ecosystem we want to exist.

If AI becomes the interface between shopper intent and purchase, the tools that help brands compete in that world should belong to a broader community than one company alone.

And yes, if we are being honest, we would also like the GitHub star badge. Not as the main reason, but as a fun signal that other builders find the project worth paying attention to.
